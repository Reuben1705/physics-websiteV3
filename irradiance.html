Irradiance

Irradiance is defined as the power per unit area incident on a surface

I =   P⁄A

Where
-I is the irradiance of radiation measure in watts per square meter.
- P is the power of the radiation incident on a surface measured in Watts.
- A is the area of the surface measured in metres squared.

In this section, we consider light being emitted from a point source.

A point source is a small source that emits radiation uniformly in all direction e.g. a small lamp.

We can establish the relationship between the irradiance and the disrance from a point source of light with an experiment:

1. Set up a clear lamp with a small filament as the point source over a black surface in a darkened room with a light sensor and ruler.

2. Measure the ambient light level with the lamp off. You will need to subtract this reading from all light measurements so that your results give light due to the lamp only.

3. Using the ruler as a reference, vary the distance, d, of the light sensor from the centre of the lamp.

4. As the area of the light sensor is constant the light sensor reading can be taken as the irradiance.

5. Plot irradiance against 1 / d2

When plotting irradiance against 1 / d2, we get a straight line through the origin.
This means irradiance is directly proportional to the inverse of the distance squared for a point source of light.
This is an inverse square law.
